Gandhiji Details





Gandhiji was a pivotal leader of India's independence movement, known for his philosophy of non-violent resistance based on truth and non-violence. On October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, he led major peaceful campaignslike the Dandi March and the Quit India Movement to end British rule. Emphasis on simplicity, self-reliance, and equality through practices like wearing khadi and boycotting foreign goods inspired millions and influenced global figures.Â 

Life and education :

  • Born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat.
  • Studied law in England and worked as a lawyer in South Africa, where he faced discrimination and began his career as a political activist.
  • Married Kasturba Makharji at age 13 and had four sons.
    •

Contribution to India's freedom struggle :

  1. Became a leader in India's independence movement after returning from South Africa.
  2. Championed peaceful protests and civil disobedience as his primary tools.
  3. Led significant movements, including the Non-Cooperation Movement, the Dandi Salt March, and the Quit India Movement.
  4. Inspired Indians to boycott British goods and embrace self-sufficiency through the use of local products and the hand-spun cloth, khadi
